# Staker: Strategy Inc (MSTR)

**A Leveraged Bitcoin Treasury Racing Its Own Obligation Clock**

Strategy Inc runs a reflexive Bitcoin treasury that has recovered from comparable mNAV compression before, but now faces $1.76 billion per year in perpetual preferred obligations and an organized adversarial campaign that did not exist in the prior cycle.

June 2026, by Vinnie Falco

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Strategy Inc holds 847,363 BTC. At approximately $60,000 per coin, the position is worth $50.8 billion. The company acquired it at an average cost of $75,651 - above current market price. The position was assembled through $6.7 billion in convertible notes and $15.5 billion in perpetual preferred stock, layered atop a declining enterprise software business generating $477 million per year.

Two competing dynamics define the current situation.

The structural thesis holds that the capital architecture has created an obligation clock. Annual preferred dividends and debt interest total $1.76 billion. Software revenue covers 28% of that. The $2.55 billion USD Reserve provides approximately 17 months of coverage. Bitcoin must recover or capital markets must remain accessible within that window. If neither occurs, the structure fails under its own weight.

The adversarial thesis holds that Wall Street actors - short sellers, plaintiff securities firms, and public critics - have identified Saylor and the BTC stash as a high-value target. The Rosen Law Firm investigation, Peter Schiff's public campaign, and 14% short interest represent coordinated pressure on a company whose founder carries two regulatory settlements.

Neither thesis operates in isolation. The structural vulnerability creates the attack surface. The adversarial pressure accelerates the structural timeline by depressing securities prices and deterring new investors.

The critical counter-evidence: mNAV compression below 1.0 is not unprecedented. In May 2022, simple mNAV hit 0.5. The company recovered to above 2.0 within two years. BTC per share - the strategy's stated metric - is up 13.3% year-to-date. The June 29, 2026 Digital Credit Capital Framework introduced adaptive measures including buyback authorizations, a BTC monetization program, and a formalized USD Reserve policy.

What differs from 2022: $1.76 billion per year in perpetual obligations (near-zero in 2022), Bitcoin ETF competition ($74 billion AUM, none in 2022), and a cost basis 27% above current price. Time was free in 2022. Time now costs $1.76 billion per year.

## 2. The Organization

Strategy Inc, formerly MicroStrategy Incorporated, was founded in 1989 by Michael Saylor and Sanju Bansal. Headquartered in Tysons Corner, Virginia. Rebranded to Strategy Inc in early 2025. Publicly traded on Nasdaq since June 1998. Employs 1,539 people, down from 2,152 in 2022.

Blau-Scott classification (Blau and Scott 1962): business concern. The declared prime beneficiary is shareholders and owners.

Governance operates through a dual-class share structure. Class B shares carry 10 votes per share. Saylor holds 19,616,680 Class B shares through Alcantara LLC - 99.9% of all Class B stock. Combined voting power: 44.1%. Economic interest: 7.4%. The company ceased Nasdaq controlled-company status in November 2024 but retains the dual-class structure with no sunset provision.

CEO and chairman roles separated since August 2022. Phong Le serves as President and CEO. Saylor holds the title Executive Chairman. The board has eight directors, six independent. Carl Rickertsen chairs the Nominating Committee established November 2024.

Saylor carries two regulatory settlements. The SEC settlement of December 2000 cost $8.28 million in disgorgement and $350,000 in penalties over accounting fraud related to premature revenue recognition. The DC Attorney General settlement of June 2024 cost $40 million over allegations of tax evasion from 2005 through 2020 - the largest income tax recovery in DC history. Neither settlement included admission of wrongdoing.

The software business generates $477 million in annual revenue. Subscription services are growing at 48-69% year-over-year as the company transitions from on-premise to cloud. Revenue has been flat to declining since peaking at $580 million in 2014.

## 3. The Landscape

### Market conditions

Bitcoin trades at approximately $60,000, down from an all-time high near $109,000 in January 2025. Fear and Greed Index stands at 18 (Extreme Fear). Bitcoin ETF outflows reached $1.79 billion in the week ending June 26.

MSTR stock trades at approximately $87, down approximately 82% from its November 2024 peak near $473. Market capitalization is approximately $28.8 billion.

### mNAV - multiple definitions

Three definitions of mNAV circulate. All yield different figures.

Simple mNAV (market cap divided by BTC value): approximately 0.57. This is the ratio most third-party analysts cite. By this measure, current compression is comparable to May 2022, when simple mNAV hit 0.5.

Enterprise mNAV (enterprise value divided by BTC value, as displayed on strategy.com): approximately 0.95. Enterprise value equals market cap plus convertible debt plus preferred notional minus USD Reserve. By this measure, sub-1.0 enterprise mNAV has not occurred previously. In the May 2022 bear trough, enterprise mNAV bottomed at approximately 1.1 because $2.25 billion in convertible debt kept enterprise value above BTC value even as the stock collapsed.

Saylor's revised mNAV (which counts the $2.55 billion USD Reserve as part of the enterprise asset base alongside Bitcoin): approximately 1.04. This definition was introduced in 2026 and yields a reading above parity.

The definitional choice determines the narrative. Sub-1.0 on the simple basis is cyclical and has precedent. Sub-1.0 on the enterprise basis is new. Above 1.0 on the revised basis supports the case that the company remains solvent and functional.

### The 2022 precedent

In May 2022, simple mNAV fell to 0.5. Bitcoin traded at $29,000. MSTR traded at $17 (split-adjusted). The company held approximately 129,000 BTC with approximately $2.25 billion in convertible debt and zero preferred stock obligations.

Recovery: within two years, mNAV exceeded 3.0 as Bitcoin rose from $16,500 to $109,000. The company's capital structure was lighter, its obligations near-zero, and no ETF competition existed. Time was free.

In 2026, the capital structure carries $15.5 billion in perpetual preferred atop $6.7 billion in convertible notes. Annual obligations total $1.76 billion. ETFs hold $74 billion in AUM. The recovery playbook has not been invalidated, but the conditions for running it have changed materially.

### BTC per share - the strategy's stated metric

Year-to-date BTC Yield as of May 25, 2026: 13.3%. BTC Gain: 89,378 bitcoin. BTC per share: 220,900 sats. The strategy is working by its own stated metric. BTC per share has increased in every year since inception, including 2026.

BTC Yield measures gross accretion. It does not subtract the cost of capital - preferred dividends, convertible interest, or dilution cost. A company can grow BTC per share while total value declines if the cost of capital exceeds the value of the BTC acquired. Whether this metric reflects genuine value creation or capital deployment masquerading as return depends on Bitcoin's long-term trajectory.

### Competitors and ecosystem

Strategy holds approximately 20 times more BTC than its nearest competitor, Twenty One Capital (43,514 BTC). Metaplanet holds 40,177 BTC. MARA Holdings holds 38,700 BTC.

Bitcoin spot ETFs: BlackRock's IBIT holds 803,000 BTC at a 0.25% expense ratio. Total ETF AUM is $74 billion. ETFs offer direct Bitcoin exposure without capital structure complexity, management risk, or perpetual obligations.

### June 29 Capital Framework

On June 29, 2026, Strategy announced a Digital Credit Capital Framework with five components: (1) a Board-approved USD Reserve policy at $2.55 billion, covering 17.4 months of obligations; (2) an increased STRC dividend rate to 12% effective July 2026; (3) a $1 billion Digital Credit Securities repurchase authorization; (4) a $1 billion Class A common stock repurchase authorization; (5) a $1.25 billion BTC Monetization Program authorizing Bitcoin sales for the first time at scale.

Combined USD Reserve and BTC monetization capacity: 25.9 months of obligations coverage.

MSTR rose 6% and STRC rose 9% on the announcement. This is adaptive management, not denial. The company acknowledged the structural pressure and created tools to address it.

## 4. Structural Assessment

### 4.1 The Obligation Clock

The defining structural feature is time pressure from permanent obligations.

Annual preferred dividends and debt interest: $1.76 billion. Software revenue: $477 million. Coverage ratio: 27%. The gap is $1.28 billion per year.

The gap must be filled by capital markets activity or BTC sales. At mNAV below 1.0 on a simple basis, equity issuance is dilutive. Preferred issuance at below-par trading prices requires higher yields to attract buyers. BTC sales reduce the asset backing all instruments.

USD Reserve: $2.55 billion. At the $1.76 billion annual run rate, this provides 17.4 months of standalone coverage. Including the $1.25 billion BTC monetization authorization, total liquidity provides approximately 25.9 months.

The clock started when the premium engine stalled. Every month that mNAV remains below 1.0 on a simple basis, the company consumes reserves that become harder to replenish. In 2022, the company had no clock. It could wait indefinitely for Bitcoin to recover because its fixed obligations were negligible. In 2026, each quarter of waiting costs approximately $440 million.

The clock is not a death sentence. It is a test with a time limit. If Bitcoin recovers above $75,000 within the reserve window, the portfolio moves above water, mNAV likely recovers above 1.0, and the equity issuance engine restarts. If not, additional reserves must come from BTC sales or higher-cost capital raises. (high confidence on mechanism; medium confidence on timeline - depends on Bitcoin trajectory)

### 4.2 The Adversarial Surface

The adversarial thesis holds that the primary threat is targeted attack, not self-destruction.

Evidence supporting the thesis:
- Rosen Law Firm opened a securities investigation June 24, 2026, examining whether Strategy issued materially misleading business information. No lawsuit filed. No wrongdoing alleged. Investigation covers MSTR and all preferred series.
- Peter Schiff publicly argued that STRC investors have an "ironclad" lawsuit against Saylor. He accused Saylor of marketing STRC to "risk-averse retirees" without proper risk disclosure. He called the structure a "death spiral."
- Short interest at approximately 14% of market cap. Goldman Sachs data: most shorted large-cap U.S. stock.
- Citron Research's November 2024 long-BTC/short-MSTR trade is profitable. MSTR declined 82% versus BTC's approximate 45% decline over the same period.
- Saylor's two regulatory settlements create a pattern that plaintiff firms can cite for credibility attacks.

Evidence weakening the thesis:
- No SEC action, DOJ case, filed complaint, or named misstatement exists as of June 30, 2026.
- Investigation notices are routine in volatile sectors. Rosen files such notices against many companies.
- Strategy's SEC prospectuses explicitly disclose that dividends are not guaranteed.
- Schiff is a known Bitcoin critic whose bearish predictions about Bitcoin itself have been consistently wrong.
- Analyst Shanaka Anslem publicly challenged the framing: "No SEC action. No Department of Justice case. No filed complaint. No named misstatement."

Assessment: the adversarial thesis is plausible but unproven. The attack surface exists because of genuine structural vulnerabilities. Whether adversarial actors can convert that surface into actionable legal claims depends on whether Saylor's marketing of STRC crossed the line from enthusiastic promotion to material misrepresentation. The SEC filings contain risk disclosures. The social media promotion was more assertive. The gap between prospectus language and Twitter promotion is the legal battleground. (medium confidence)

### 4.3 The Reflexive Engine

The premium engine operates symmetrically. Above mNAV 1.0, equity issuance buys Bitcoin accretively, increasing BTC per share, which attracts capital, which sustains the premium. Below mNAV 1.0, the same mechanism reverses: equity issuance is dilutive, the market reads dilution as distress, and the premium compresses further (Soros 1987).

The engine reversed in late June 2026. Enterprise mNAV fell below 1.0 - a first on the EV basis, though simple mNAV hit 0.5 in 2022. Simple mNAV reached approximately 0.57.

Historical performance of the engine:
- August 2020 to November 2024: engine ran in positive direction. mNAV peaked near 3.1 (enterprise basis). BTC per share compounded at 69% annually.
- May 2022: engine stalled. Simple mNAV hit 0.5. Engine restarted within two years as Bitcoin recovered.
- June 2026: engine stalled again. Different structural context.

The question: can the engine restart?

Arguments for restart: Bitcoin is cyclical. Extreme fear readings historically precede recoveries. The company retains capital markets access through preferred and ATM programs. BTC per share continues to accrete.

Arguments against restart: ETF competition has permanently reduced the scarcity premium that supported mNAV above 1.0. In 2022, MSTR was the primary equity-market Bitcoin vehicle. In 2026, IBIT alone approaches Strategy's holdings. The premium's natural equilibrium may now be at or below 1.0.

Both arguments have merit. The outcome depends on whether the premium was driven by structural scarcity (bearish for restart) or by leveraged exposure appeal (still relevant for risk-seeking investors who want more-than-1x Bitcoin). (medium confidence on either direction)

### 4.4 The Key-Man Paradox

Saylor is simultaneously the strategy's irreplaceable engine and its primary attack surface (Bolton, Wang and Yang 2019).

As engine: Saylor conceived the strategy. His media presence, conference appearances, and social media activity recruit new investors. The "digital gold" narrative is his. No one at the company can replicate this function.

As attack surface: two regulatory settlements, a distinctive public persona, and 44.1% voting control make Saylor the cheapest target for adversaries. The dual-class structure means governance cannot remove him - which channels all pressure into non-governance mechanisms: litigation, regulatory complaints, and reputational campaigns (Eldar 2018). These are harder to defend and more damaging to market confidence than proxy fights.

The paradox is real. If Saylor's credibility holds, he can rebuild the premium narrative as Bitcoin recovers. If his credibility erodes through litigation or regulatory action, the premium cannot recover regardless of Bitcoin's price.

No succession plan exists. Saylor is 61. The entire capital architecture depends on his continued participation and credibility. (high confidence on dependency; medium confidence on near-term trigger risk)

### 4.5 The Governance Lock

The dual-class structure prevents capital allocation correction through normal governance channels (Bebchuk, Kraakman and Triantis 2000).

Saylor's 44.1% voting power cannot be overridden without extraordinary coordination among the remaining shareholders. Passive index funds hold approximately 18-20% and vote with management by default. Active institutional shareholders who object can only exit. Voice is structurally foreclosed; only exit remains (Hirschman 1970).

The governance lock is not inherently destructive. If the strategy is correct - if Bitcoin appreciates sufficiently - the lock protects long-term execution from short-term panic. Entrenchment is only a cost when the controller is wrong. The lock becomes a liability only in a scenario where the capital structure fails and no internal constituency can force correction.

The company's loss of Nasdaq controlled-company status in November 2024 triggered some governance reforms. The Nominating and Compensation Committees now consist solely of independent directors. But Saylor's voting bloc ensures these reforms have limited practical impact on strategic direction. (high confidence)

### 4.6 The Capital Structure Ratchet

Perpetual preferred stock creates a one-way ratchet that distinguishes 2026 from 2022 (Myers 1984).

Convertible notes mature. The 2027 Notes (~$1.01 billion) come due in February 2027, creating a hard deadline. If the stock is below conversion prices, the company must repay in cash or refinance. The 2029 Notes have been partially retired ($1.5 billion repurchased at an 8% discount in May 2026).

Perpetual preferred does not mature. There is no obligation to repay principal. But there is an obligation to pay dividends - cumulative on four of five series. If dividends are suspended, they accrue and must be paid before common shareholders receive anything.

The ratchet is self-tightening. Each new preferred share issued permanently adds to the annual obligation. The $15.5 billion notional was issued when mNAV was well above 1.0. The obligation is permanent. The premium was not.

However: the June 29 Capital Framework introduced counter-ratchet mechanisms. The $1 billion Digital Credit repurchase authorization allows the company to retire preferred at discounts to par, reducing annual obligations. If STRF can be repurchased at $71-75 (versus $100 par), each repurchased share eliminates $10 per year in dividends at 71-75% of the cost. This is accretive. The company recognized this and acted.

The question is whether the buyback authorization ($1 billion) is sufficient relative to the total preferred notional ($15.5 billion). It is not. But it creates directionality: the company can now reduce obligations rather than only increase them. This is new. (high confidence on mechanism; medium confidence on whether buybacks will be large enough to matter)

### 4.7 The Commons Gap

Strategy holds 4.2% of total Bitcoin supply and has contributed zero dollars to Bitcoin Core development. Bitcoin Core operates on approximately $8 million per year. Five maintainers control merge access. Strategy's $50.8 billion position depends entirely on their continued work (Ostrom 1990).

Saylor has publicly described developers as "the greatest risk" to Bitcoin. He advocates protocol ossification and has allegedly discouraged at least one institutional funder (ARK Invest) from supporting development. The ARK incident is disputed: Matt Odell alleged Saylor "actively killed deals"; Samson Mow rebutted that Saylor "simply expressed his opinions." Cathie Wood ultimately committed to recurring donations.

The commons gap is real. The zero-contribution fact is verified across four independent sources listing Bitcoin Core funders. Whether this constitutes harmful free-riding depends on whether Bitcoin Core's funding model is resilient to individual non-participation. Current funding comes from Brink ($7.8 million in 2025), OpenSats ($23 million lifetime), Spiral, and other sources. Strategy's absence is notable but not existentially threatening to Core's operations.

The selection effect is more concerning. When the holder of 4.2% of supply publicly brands developers as threats, this shapes who enters and remains in Core development. The long-term consequences are uncertain. (high confidence on zero-contribution fact; medium confidence on chilling effect; low confidence on long-term selection effects)

### Domain-specific findings

**Reflexive Premium Engine.** The reflexive engine has reversed on a simple-mNAV basis, with the ratio at approximately 0.57. On the enterprise-mNAV basis, it is at approximately 0.95. The engine reversed similarly in 2022 (simple mNAV hit 0.5) and recovered within two years. The reversal is cyclical by historical precedent. Whether it is terminal depends on whether ETF competition has permanently eliminated the premium's structural foundation. This question cannot be resolved in real time. (medium confidence on terminal vs. cyclical)

**Negative Activism Vulnerability.** Rosen Law Firm investigation, Schiff public campaign, 14% short interest, and two prior regulatory settlements create an attack surface (Molk and Partnoy 2022). No legal action has been filed. The attack surface is wide but unproven as actionable. Investigation notices are common in volatile sectors. (medium confidence)

**Capital Structure Irreversibility.** The perpetual preferred obligations total $1.76 billion per year with no maturity. Unlike 2022, when the company carried only convertible debt that could be retired, the perpetual preferred is structurally irreversible absent market repurchases. The June 29 buyback authorization addresses this partially. Bitcoin appreciation above $150,000 would make the obligations trivial relative to portfolio value (1.3% of holdings). The trap is real only if Bitcoin does not appreciate sufficiently, and no framework can predict Bitcoin's trajectory. (high confidence on mechanism; inherently uncertain on outcome)

**Key-Man as Narrative Engine and Attack Surface.** Saylor functions as both the irreplaceable driver and the primary adversarial target. No succession plan exists. The 44.1% voting control channels all adversarial pressure into non-governance mechanisms. (high confidence)

**Adversarial Senior Claims Cascade.** The coexistence of convertible notes, perpetual preferred, and common equity creates structurally adversarial relationships among capital classes (Hart and Moore 1995). Preferred holders need asset preservation. Equity holders need aggressive accumulation. Every strategic decision that benefits one class harms the other. STRF trading at $71-75 (25-29% below par) already signals distress pricing. (high confidence)

**CEF Premium Mean-Reversion.** The mNAV premium followed closed-end fund dynamics (Lee, Shleifer and Thaler 1991). The 2022 recovery falsified the one-way mean-reversion prediction once: the premium reverted to a discount and then recovered. Cyclicality, not terminal reversion, remains plausible. The difference from a pure CEF: the $1.76 billion annual obligation makes a persistent discount existential rather than benign. (high confidence on mechanism; medium confidence on terminal vs. cyclical)

**Dual-Class Entrenchment.** The Class B 10:1 structure has no sunset provision. Saylor's control is permanent absent voluntary relinquishment. This channels adversarial energy into litigation and reputational campaigns rather than governance (Eldar 2018). The entrenchment is value-preserving if the strategy is correct. It is value-destructive if the strategy fails and no correction mechanism exists. (high confidence on structure; inherently uncertain on whether strategy is correct)

**Commons Parasitism.** Zero contribution to Bitcoin Core development while holding 4.2% of supply. Developer selection effects from Saylor's public framing. The dependency is absolute: there is no alternative Bitcoin network. Current Core funding appears sufficient despite Strategy's non-participation. (high confidence on fact; medium confidence on consequences)

**Narrative Contagion.** The 82% stock decline exceeds Bitcoin's approximately 45% decline. The excess measures narrative premium evaporation (Shiller 2019). Narratives exhibit phase-transition behavior. The Rosen investigation is a narrative event as much as a legal one. Whether the narrative can be rebuilt depends on Bitcoin's trajectory and Saylor's credibility. (high confidence on mechanism; medium confidence on trajectory)

**Competitive Displacement.** Bitcoin ETFs are structurally superior for every investor use case except leveraged speculation (Gorton and Metrick 2012). IBIT holds 803,000 BTC at 0.25% expense ratio. Strategy holds 847,363 BTC with $1.76 billion in annual obligations. The displacement is permanent. The question is pace: gradual displacement allows Strategy time to adapt; rapid displacement accelerates the obligation clock. (high confidence on direction; medium confidence on pace)

## 5. Cui Bono

The stated beneficiary is shareholders. Strategy is a business concern. The declared prime beneficiary is shareholders and owners.

The control-to-ownership gap is measurable. Saylor holds 44.1% of voting power and 7.4% of economic interest. This 6:1 ratio means Saylor bears $0.074 of every dollar of value destruction while retaining $0.441 of every unit of governance power. The structure underweights Saylor's exposure to downside.

However: Saylor holds 17,732 BTC personally (approximately $1.1 billion at current prices). His personal wealth is substantially tied to Bitcoin's success. His incentive alignment with the Bitcoin thesis is genuine even if his governance exposure to MSTR-specific risk is diluted.

Secondary beneficiaries:
- Coinbase earns custody fees on an estimated $17 billion in Bitcoin with a $100 million liability cap. Brian Brooks (former Coinbase CLO) sits on Strategy's board.
- Investment banks earned underwriting fees on $22 billion or more in securities issuance. Fees are non-refundable.
- Market makers profit from MSTR volatility and options activity regardless of direction.
- Plaintiff law firms profit from securities investigations and class actions regardless of outcome.

Primary risk bearers:
- Preferred holders ($15.5 billion par, approximately 80% retail) hold perpetual instruments at 22-29% below par with no governance rights.
- Common shareholders have experienced an 82% decline from ATH with no governance remedy.
- Employees (1,539) face career risk at a company whose primary asset is volatile.

## 6. Power Dynamics

### Key bilateral relationships

**Saylor to the board.** Saylor's 44.1% voting power makes the relationship unidirectional. The board ratifies. Since November 2024, the Nominating and Compensation Committees are independently chaired. This is a governance improvement but does not alter the fundamental power dynamic.

**Strategy to capital markets.** The relationship has inverted. During the premium era, Strategy commanded favorable terms from banks eager for issuance mandates. At mNAV below 1.0, any new issuance is dilutive or costly. The company's June 29 announcement that it will pause equity issuance near 1x mNAV acknowledges this inversion.

**Strategy to Coinbase.** Coinbase custodies an estimated 40% of Strategy's Bitcoin under a $100 million liability cap. The dependence is asymmetric (Emerson 1962): Strategy cannot easily relocate $17 billion in BTC. Coinbase could survive losing the account. Arkham Intelligence traced approximately $5.1 billion moving from Coinbase to Fidelity Custody in 2025, suggesting diversification is underway.

**Strategy to Bitcoin Core.** Pure dependency with zero reciprocity. Strategy extracts $50.8 billion in stored value from the network. Core operates on $8 million per year. Saylor frames the relationship as adversarial by calling developers the greatest risk.

**Preferred holders to management.** No governance mechanism connects preferred holders to decision-making. The $1.76 billion annual claim has no governance representation. The Rosen investigation may be the first attempt to create leverage through litigation what governance cannot provide.

**Short sellers to Strategy.** Adversarial capital with disclosed positions. Citron and Kerrisdale published short theses. The short interest at 14% of market cap represents active adversarial positioning. Short sellers profit from exactly the narrative destruction that harms all other stakeholders.

### Coalitions

**The Saylor control coalition** (Saylor 44.1% + passive index funds approximately 18-20% + aligned retail). This coalition controls governance outcomes on every vote. Two of three elements are structurally fixed.

**The adversarial coalition** (short sellers + plaintiff firms + Schiff-aligned critics). Loosely coordinated through public statements and information campaigns. No formal structure. United by the thesis that the capital structure will fail and the securities are overvalued.

**The preferred holder non-coalition.** Approximately 80% retail, dispersed, with no communication channel and no governance rights. This group should organize but cannot. The Rosen investigation is the first attempt to aggregate this group's interests through contingency-fee litigation.

### Structural holes

**Preferred holders to the board.** No governance channel connects the $15.5 billion preferred claim to board-level oversight.

**Strategy to Bitcoin Core developers.** No formal relationship between the largest corporate holder and the project securing its holdings.

## 9. Predictions

### Short-term (0-12 months)

If Bitcoin recovers above $75,000 by Q1 2027, the portfolio moves above water. mNAV likely recovers above 1.0 on a simple basis. Equity issuance becomes accretive again. The obligation clock pauses. Preferred securities recover toward par. If not, the company consumes approximately $1.28 billion of the reserve gap per year, drawing down the $2.55 billion reserve and potentially exercising the $1.25 billion BTC monetization authorization. (high confidence on mechanism)

If the Rosen investigation produces a filed class action within 6 months, legal costs and reputational damage accelerate the adversarial dynamic. STRC and STRF trade lower on litigation risk. If no lawsuit materializes, the investigation fades as a narrative event. (medium confidence - investigation notices frequently do not produce viable lawsuits)

If STRC remains below $85 and the 12% dividend rate proves insufficient to stabilize the price, Strategy faces a choice: raise the rate further (increasing the annual obligation) or accept trading at a persistent discount to par (impairing future issuance capacity). Neither option is free. (medium-high confidence)

### Medium-term (1-3 years)

If Bitcoin ETF AUM exceeds $150 billion and IBIT surpasses Strategy's BTC holdings, the scale argument disappears. Strategy becomes the second-largest corporate holder behind an ETF charging 0.25% per year. The market reclassifies MSTR from leveraged Bitcoin play to overleveraged holding company trading at a conglomerate discount. If not - if ETF growth stalls or reverses - MSTR retains its unique positioning. (medium confidence on direction; low confidence on timing)

If the company fails to meet preferred obligations in any quarter, cumulative dividends accrue. Legal standing for breach-of-contract claims crystallizes. Class action litigation follows. In a restructuring, convertible holders recover first, preferred holders receive a fraction, and equity is severely diluted. If obligations continue to be met - as they have been for 23 consecutive distributions totaling $693 million - the capital structure demonstrates sustainability that the market eventually reprices. (medium confidence on either scenario)

If Bitcoin enters a sustained multi-year bull market (above $100,000), the obligation clock becomes irrelevant. At $150,000, the 847,363 BTC portfolio would be worth $127 billion. The $1.76 billion annual obligation would be 1.4% of holdings value. The premium likely recovers. BTC per share accretion accelerates. The strategy vindicated. This is the scenario the capital structure was designed for. (medium confidence - Bitcoin trajectory inherently uncertain)

### Long-term (3-7 years)

If Bitcoin stagnates below $70,000 for multiple years, the obligation clock eventually exhausts all reserve and monetization capacity. Restructuring becomes the only path. The 847,363 BTC is the primary restructuring asset. Forced sale of 4.2% of supply during distress would depress Bitcoin prices, creating a reflexive downward spiral affecting all Bitcoin holders. (medium confidence - depends on sustained low Bitcoin environment, which contradicts Bitcoin's historical cyclical behavior)

If Saylor departs - voluntarily, through health, or through regulatory action - within 5 years, the simultaneous loss of narrative engine, governance anchor, and public identity triggers repricing. Whether the repricing is negative (premium collapse) or positive (potential ETF conversion, Bitcoin distribution, or structural simplification) depends on the successor's strategy. Saylor's departure is not necessarily destructive if the successor unlocks value through structural change. (medium confidence on consequences; low confidence on trigger probability)
