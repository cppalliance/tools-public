# The Internal Revenue Service: Fiscal Foundation in Structural Decline

**The institution that generates $420 for every dollar appropriated is being simultaneously defunded, depopulated, and depiloted - while its automated systems continue processing returns on schedule.**

June 2026, by Vinnie Falco

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The IRS is in the most severe structural degradation of its 164-year history. Three compound dynamics drive the decline: a revenue doom loop where cutting the agency's enforcement capacity reduces federal revenue, which increases deficits, which generates political pressure for further cuts; a knowledge extinction spiral where the tacit expertise needed to maintain critical systems, execute complex enforcement, and operate filing seasons is departing in a compressed cohort with no replacement pipeline; and a metrics opacity shield where every mechanism that would make the degradation visible to principals and the public has been severed, redefined, or masked by corporate management language.

The dominant dynamic is the revenue doom loop, because breaking it would improve the most other findings. But Pierson's retrenchment theory explains why it cannot break from within the current political structure: the costs of IRS degradation are diffuse (spread across all taxpayers and government services), delayed (3-5 year lag between enforcement cuts and measurable revenue loss), and invisible (appearing as deficits rather than as IRS-specific losses). No constituency perceives itself as an IRS beneficiary because the agency's value proposition - collecting the money that funds everything - is structurally invisible to voters.

The prognosis is Abandoned. The IRS was functional as recently as 2023, when Inflation Reduction Act investment was narrowing the gap between mission and capacity. It is now pilotless, with no Senate-confirmed Commissioner, an unauthorized "CEO" simultaneously running the Social Security Administration, and 28% of its workforce eliminated in twelve months. Core tax collection continues on automated momentum and voluntary compliance inertia. But the institutional capacity for complex enforcement, IT modernization, and self-renewal is being consumed. If the current trajectory holds for three to five years, several compound dynamics will cross irreversibility thresholds - not because the IRS ceases to exist, but because the capacity to rebuild it will have been consumed alongside the institution itself.

## 2. The Subject

The Internal Revenue Service was established in 1862 as the Bureau of Internal Revenue to fund the Civil War, renamed in 1952, and restructured by the IRS Restructuring and Reform Act of 1998.<sup>1</sup> It is a bureau within the U.S. Department of the Treasury, led by a Commissioner of Internal Revenue appointed by the President and confirmed by the Senate for a five-year term.

The agency collects approximately $4.7-5.1 trillion in gross federal revenue annually - roughly 96% of the federal government's operating income.<sup>2</sup> It processes over 150 million individual tax returns per year, administers social programs worth over $100 billion through the tax code (Earned Income Tax Credit, Child Tax Credit, Affordable Care Act premium credits), and provides the economic data that underpins GDP calculations and fiscal policy modeling.

The IRS operates through four major divisions: Wage & Investment (individual taxpayers), Small Business/Self-Employed (small businesses and partnerships), Large Business & International (corporations and high-wealth), and Tax-Exempt & Government Entities (nonprofits and pensions). Criminal Investigation handles tax fraud and financial crimes. The Taxpayer Advocate Service provides independent assistance and systemic advocacy. Two Enterprise Computing Centers (Martinsburg, WV and Memphis, TN) constitute the entire data center infrastructure.

The stated mission: "Provide America's taxpayers top-quality service by helping them understand and meet their tax responsibilities and enforce the law with integrity and fairness to all."

As of June 2026, the Commissioner position is vacant. Frank Bisignano, a former fintech CEO with no tax administration background, holds the unauthorized title of "CEO" while simultaneously heading the Social Security Administration.<sup>3</sup> Seven different commissioners or acting commissioners served in 2025 alone.<sup>4</sup> The workforce has been reduced from approximately 102,000 to 75,700 in twelve months - a 28% net reduction.<sup>5</sup>

## 3. The Landscape

The U.S. tax system operates on voluntary compliance: taxpayers calculate their own liability and remit payment. The voluntary compliance rate has held at approximately 85% for decades, meaning the system's revenue capacity is a function of public trust, perceived fairness, and credible enforcement rather than physical coercion (Kirchler, Hoelzl, and Wahl 2008). When any of these erode, revenue declines without any law changing.

The tax gap - the difference between taxes owed and taxes collected - stands at $696 billion gross ($606 billion net after enforcement recoveries) for TY2022, representing approximately 13% of total tax liability.<sup>6</sup> Underreporting by self-employed individuals, partnerships, and complex entities constitutes the majority. These categories require resource-intensive field audits to address; they cannot be detected by automated systems matching third-party information reports (Allingham and Sandmo 1972).

The IRS occupies a government monopoly on federal tax collection, with a regulated ecosystem of private intermediaries. The tax preparation industry ($15 billion+ market) operates as licensed participants: H&R Block, Intuit/TurboTax, Jackson Hewitt, and over 700,000 enrolled agents, CPAs, and attorneys. The IRS is simultaneously a monopoly collector and a monopsony buyer of compliance labor, competing against private-sector employers offering two to four times federal salary for the same specializations.

Congressional funding controls are the binding constraint. The IRS cannot self-fund. It depends entirely on annual appropriations plus supplemental funding. Congress has whipsawed the agency between starvation (2010-2021: 26% inflation-adjusted enforcement cuts), sudden investment (IRA 2022: $79.4 billion over ten years), and back to starvation (2024-2026: $53.5 billion rescinded from IRA).<sup>7</sup> Each $1 invested in enforcement historically returns $5-12 in revenue.<sup>2</sup>

The legacy IT infrastructure creates a hard ceiling on modernization speed. The Individual Master File (IMF), dating to 1962 and written in assembly language, remains the authoritative data source for every individual tax account in America. Virtually every IRS system depends on it. Replacement has been attempted since at least 2009 (CADE, CADE-2) and repeatedly stalled. The current target completion date is "unknown."<sup>8</sup> 63% of IRS IT systems are classified as legacy.<sup>9</sup>

The threat landscape is worsening independently of institutional capacity: AI-enabled fraud increases sophistication and volume, cryptocurrency creates opaque asset classes, partnership structures proliferate as sheltering vehicles, and Congress continues to add tax code complexity (the One Big Beautiful Bill Act of 2025 adds over 100 provisions, many retroactive).<sup>10</sup> The agency's adversaries adopt technology without constraint while the IRS is locked into 1962-era core architecture.

## 4. Structural Assessment

### 4.1 The Revenue Inversion

The IRS generates approximately $420 for every $1 appropriated - the highest return on investment in the federal government.<sup>2</sup> CBO has scored IRS enforcement investment as revenue-positive: the $20.2 billion FY2024 rescission alone was projected to lose $44 billion in revenue over a decade.<sup>11</sup> Yale Budget Lab estimates the combined staffing and funding cuts will increase federal deficits by $600-861 billion over 2026-2035.<sup>12</sup> The Center for American Progress, incorporating deterrence decay effects that persist for fourteen or more years, estimates $909 billion.<sup>13</sup>

The structural picture is a fiscal death spiral. Enforcement capacity is cut, which reduces the probability of detection for tax evaders, which widens the tax gap, which increases deficits, which generates political pressure for spending cuts, which falls on the IRS (Allingham and Sandmo 1972). The spiral's key feature is that the revenue losses appear as deficits rather than as IRS-specific failures, preventing attribution to the cuts that caused them. This is precisely the condition under which retrenchment succeeds: costs dispersed across a large population, manifesting with a lag, preventing blame attribution to the politicians who enacted them (Pierson 1994).

The numbers are concrete. IRA enforcement funding has been cut from $45.6 billion to $3.8 billion - a 92% rescission.<sup>7</sup> FY2025 enforcement revenue declined approximately $5 billion (roughly 5%) according to FOIA analysis.<sup>14</sup> Audit volume hit a historic low of 497,541 in FY2025, compared to an annual average of 1.7 million in 2010-2012.<sup>5</sup> The FY2027 budget proposes cutting another 1,110 enforcement positions while Congressional appropriations drop 12.2% to $9.8 billion.<sup>2</sup> Enforcement FTE is projected to fall from 32,350 to 22,728 by FY2027 - a 30% decline.<sup>2</sup>

Automated correspondence audits continue regardless of staffing - matching W-2 and 1099 data against filed returns, catching mismatches in income that third-party reporting already makes visible. But the $539 billion underreporting gap is concentrated in income types that lack third-party reporting: self-employment, partnerships, rental income, and S-corporations.<sup>6</sup> These require human examination of business records by revenue agents who take 12-18 months to train and years to become proficient. Automated systems can flag these returns; they cannot audit them. The enforcement posture is bifurcating: near-perfect visibility into wage income, declining capacity to penetrate complex entities - the exact inverse of where the tax gap concentrates.

The IRS used $4.8 billion in IRA one-time funds for routine FY2025 operations because appropriations were insufficient for basic functioning.<sup>9</sup> This is not investment - it is reserve consumption. Remaining IRA funds are projected exhausted by FY2028.<sup>7</sup> When they run out, the agency will operate on annual appropriations alone, at levels insufficient to maintain current degraded capacity. (medium-high)

### 4.2 Knowledge Extinction Behind a Functional Facade

Five independent findings from four diagnostic clusters converge on a single outcome: irreversible institutional knowledge loss. Intellectual dark matter - tacit knowledge that cannot be extracted to documentation - exists in at least three critical domains (Polanyi 1966). Assembly-language IMF specialists maintain the system that processes every individual tax account in America; their expertise cannot be hired from the market because the market does not produce assembly-language programmers for 1962-era mainframe architecture. Complex enforcement judgment - knowing which transactions to scrutinize in a partnership return, how to interpret novel sheltering structures, when a position is defensible - takes five to ten years of practice beyond formal training and resists formalization. Filing season operational knowledge - what breaks, what to watch for, how to triage 150 million returns through aging systems with temporary staff - lives in experienced operations managers.

The holders of this dark matter are departing on a compressed timeline. Voluntary Early Retirement Authority and Voluntary Separation Incentive Payment programs structurally selected for the most experienced employees - those with retirement eligibility and marketable skills (Lewis 2008). TIGTA data shows the highest-impact departures concentrated in Small Business/Self-Employed (-37%), IT (-42% including reassignments), and the Human Capital Office (-30%).<sup>5</sup> 31,273 employees separated between January 2025 and January 2026. Backfill hiring totaled 2,287, of which 2,238 were entry-level contact representatives - no replacement of specialized enforcement, IT, or technical expertise.<sup>5</sup>

The succession mechanisms that would normally capture departing knowledge have been simultaneously disabled. The career executive layer that manages knowledge transfer has been decimated: 60% loss in Chief Technology Officer staff, 55% in Taxpayer Experience Office, 48% in Procurement.<sup>8</sup> GAO found the staff reductions were "not targeted or strategic."<sup>8</sup> The IRSU training infrastructure shows a "troubling decline" in effectiveness with fragmented design and inconsistent quality.<sup>15</sup> No updated strategic workforce plan exists.<sup>8</sup>

The facade persists because core tax collection functions run on automated systems that do not require the departing expertise. Returns are processed. Refunds go out. The 85% voluntary compliance rate carries the revenue stream independently of institutional capacity. The filing season was declared "one of the most successful in recent memory" by TIGTA<sup>9</sup> and "the most successful filing season in history" by the CEO<sup>3</sup> - while the National Taxpayer Advocate documented that call answer rates fell from 25% to 21%, average hold times doubled from 8 to 14 minutes, 14 million returns were suspended during processing, and over one million taxpayers waited an average of 5.5 weeks beyond normal processing time for refunds.<sup>15</sup>

Path dependence theory predicts that the knowledge loss will not be symmetric - that once the population of knowledge-holders falls below a critical mass, the system enters a zone where it can neither be maintained nor replaced (Arthur 1989). The IRS does not publicly report the count of remaining IMF-qualified assembly-language specialists. The threshold may already have been crossed.

### 4.3 An Unpiloted Institution Under Political Capture

The IRS has no confirmed leader exercising statutory authority. Commissioner Daniel Werfel resigned January 20, 2025. Multiple acting commissioners followed. Billy Long was confirmed by the Senate in June 2025 and removed by the President on August 8, 2025 - less than two months in office. Treasury Secretary Bessent served as acting Commissioner until his FVRA authority expired on March 6, 2026, with no successor nominated.<sup>4</sup> Frank Bisignano was appointed "CEO" in October 2025 - a title Congress never created, carrying no statutory authority under the IRS Restructuring and Reform Act of 1998, and held by someone simultaneously heading the Social Security Administration.<sup>3</sup> Senate Democrats called the arrangement "a fake job" designed to circumvent Senate confirmation.<sup>4</sup>

This is not merely a vacancy. The institutional design theory of political control identifies appointment procedures as a key mechanism for aligning agent behavior with principal preferences (McCubbins, Noll, and Weingast 1987). Senate confirmation forces information disclosure about qualifications, creates a public commitment mechanism, and establishes accountability to the confirming body. The unauthorized CEO bypasses every function: no screening, no fixed term, no independent authority, no accountability to Congress separate from the executive branch. Bisignano exercises the Commissioner's statutory duties without the statutory framework designed to constrain and inform that exercise.

The capture extends below the CEO level. Todd Newnam, from the DOGE team at Treasury, was appointed CFO. Gary Shapley, a whistleblower in the Biden tax investigation, was elevated to deputy chief of Criminal Investigation. Joseph Ziegler was named to a newly created "internal consulting" role. Skills assessments were imposed on over 1,300 IT workers by political leadership.<sup>14</sup> Sixteen executives now report directly to the CEO, centralizing authority in a non-confirmed leader with divided attention between two agencies managing $6.5 trillion in combined annual flows.<sup>3</sup>

The compound dynamics are mutually reinforcing. Dead player status (no institutional adaptive capacity) prevents resistance to capture. Capture prevents a legitimate leader from emerging. Multi-principal conflict (statutory mission vs. executive directive to shrink vs. congressional mandates for new implementation) produces paralysis in strategic functions while operational momentum carries routine functions forward. The IRS "neither agreed nor disagreed" with GAO's three critical workforce planning recommendations<sup>8</sup> - the institutional response of an agent unable to serve any principal coherently.

The Anti-Weaponization Fund illustrates the capture's depth. The IRS settled the president's lawsuit against his own agency (Trump v. IRS), directing $1.776 billion from the Treasury judgment fund to a new account. Bisignano signed off in his IRS CEO capacity.<sup>16</sup> Federal courts subsequently enjoined disbursements. The settlement instrumentalized IRS litigation for political resource allocation - the agency's legal function serving the political apparatus rather than the statutory mission. (medium-high)

### 4.4 The Individual Master File: Infrastructure Locked Between Death and Replacement

The Individual Master File is a 1962-era system written in assembly language that serves as the authoritative data source for every individual tax account in America. Every IRS application depends on it directly or indirectly. During peak filing season, it processes over 13 million returns per day through the Martinsburg, WV Enterprise Computing Center.<sup>9</sup> There is no modern replacement. There is no redundant backup. A catastrophic failure would halt all individual tax processing nationwide.

The system has survived every replacement attempt. CADE (2004-2009) was abandoned. CADE-2 (2009-present) was paused in March 2025 along with all 23 modernization programs.<sup>8</sup> The current target completion date for IMF replacement is "unknown."<sup>8</sup> No legacy systems were decommissioned despite years of effort.<sup>9</sup> The 23 programs were replaced by a draft framework of 9 "targeted initiatives" with no implementation details, no timeline, and no strategic vision.<sup>8</sup> The FY2027 budget eliminates the business systems modernization appropriation line entirely.<sup>2</sup>

This is textbook technological lock-in amplified by increasing returns to the incumbent system (Arthur 1989). Each year of continued IMF operation adds dependent systems, trains remaining staff on IMF-specific processes, and deepens organizational routines around its constraints. Each failed replacement attempt increases institutional skepticism about migration. The switching costs are not decreasing - they are rising, because the workforce that could manage a transition is shrinking (IT staff reduced 42% including reassignments)<sup>14</sup> while the interconnected dependency graph grows.

The technology doom loop operates across clusters. IMF lock-in requires specialized maintenance (assembly-language dark matter held by an aging cohort). The monopoly salary paradox prevents recruiting replacements at a 2-4x compensation gap. AI-enhanced fraud accelerates the threat against a system locked into 1962-era architecture. Capacity constraints prevent response. The loop: cannot modernize (lock-in) - cannot recruit maintainers (salary gap) - existing maintainers depart (aging cliff) - system becomes harder to maintain - modernization becomes more urgent - still cannot modernize.

Besley and Persson's fiscal capacity model identifies the complementarity that makes this worse than it appears: IT capacity and enforcement capacity are complements whose joint decline exceeds the sum of individual declines (Besley and Persson 2009). Fewer agents mean less use of the systems; less IT support means agents are less effective. Both are declining simultaneously, and each decline reduces the marginal return to maintaining the other. (medium-high)

### 4.5 A Workforce Selected for Tolerance of Decline

The IRS workforce is experiencing classic evaporative cooling: the highest-value employees depart first because they have the best outside alternatives, systematically degrading the average capability of the remaining pool (Akerlof 1970). Voluntary early retirement and separation incentive programs explicitly selected for this dynamic - employees who qualified had both retirement eligibility (experience) and marketable skills (capability). Big 4 accounting firms, law practices, and fintech companies absorb departed IRS specialists at premium compensation, confirming that the departures are adversely selected.

Each departure makes the next more likely. Remaining staff carry increased workloads with fewer mentors. The working environment degrades. Institutional knowledge thins. The agency's reputation as a stable employer - already damaged by seven leaders in one year and 28% workforce reduction - deters high-quality applicants. The salary gap (2-4x between federal and private sector for comparable positions) was a structural barrier before the reductions; it is now compounded by brand damage and instability (Lewis 2008).

The external talent supply is independently contracting. Accounting undergraduate enrollment rose 8.9% in spring 2026 after prior declines, but degree completions continue falling (6.6% drop in 2023-24).<sup>17</sup> 75% of active CPAs are nearing retirement age.<sup>17</sup> The profession shows signs of pipeline recovery, but the IRS's access to that pipeline is worsening: federal hiring freezes, compressed training, and political instability make the agency a less attractive destination than the private sector even when candidates exist.

The competence trap is the predicted end-state: a stable low equilibrium where the institution lacks the competence to recognize or correct its own incompetence (Lewis 2008). The compound picture - evaporative cooling (T22), fractured pipeline (T15), contracting external supply (T34), prestige misallocation signaling that expertise is devalued (T12), and the monopoly salary paradox (T16) - has no internal equilibrium point. Each departure makes the next more likely. The spiral continues until either external intervention reverses it or the institution reaches a staffing level where only routine automated functions are executable.

## 5. Institutional Durability

The prognosis is Abandoned. The IRS was a functional institution as recently as 2023, when IRA-funded investment was narrowing the gap between statutory mission and operational capacity - hiring enforcement staff, initiating modernization programs, improving taxpayer service metrics, and developing a strategic operating plan.<sup>18</sup> It had a confirmed Commissioner (Daniel Werfel), a published roadmap, and the largest single investment in its history. The social technologies were documented, understood, and being actively maintained. The institution was a live player, adapting to its environment.

It is now pilotless. Every institutional-health test except Niche (the function is real and irreplaceable) and Ecosystem Position (extreme net provider at 420:1) produced findings, and every finding is directionally degrading. Social technology ceremonies persist without comprehension (Polanyi 1966). Entropy resistance has collapsed - training disrupted, knowledge transfer impossible during mass departure, all modernization paused, no strategic plan in force (Katz and Kahn 1966). Legitimacy is depreciating across functional, procedural, and political dimensions while legal authority remains formally intact (Suchman 1995). Detection mechanisms (TIGTA, NTA, GAO) accurately identify every dimension of the decline but correction is severed - the political principals driving the dysfunction receive the oversight reports and continue the trajectory. The self-correction system is a monitoring instrument on an autopilot aircraft with no one at the controls.

The buffer preventing terminal classification is the 85% voluntary compliance rate - the inertia of a population habituated to filing and paying taxes regardless of institutional capacity. If both enforcement power and public trust continue declining simultaneously, the Kirchler slippery slope framework predicts nonlinear compliance deterioration (Kirchler, Hoelzl, and Wahl 2008). The VCR is a lagging indicator measured with a 3-5 year delay. By the time a decline is measurable, the structural conditions will have been in place for years. The institution's statutory permanence is the final mask: the IRS will continue to exist, process returns, and issue refunds long after the capacity for complex enforcement, technology modernization, and institutional renewal has been lost. The Ottoman tax apparatus maintained its forms for decades after the capacity to execute them had degraded - the IRS's automated systems will sustain the illusion more efficiently.

## 6. Predictions

**Short-term (1-2 years)**

1. If the 2027 filing season is executed on the current workforce and IT trajectory, processing delays will exceed 2026 levels and service degradation will become politically visible. The One Big Beautiful Bill Act adds over 100 tax code changes (many retroactive) requiring programming, guidance, and form updates on 60-year-old systems with a 27% smaller workforce.<sup>10</sup> If emergency reinvestment occurs before August 2026 (seasonal hiring deadline), delays will stabilize but not improve. Confidence: medium-high (current trajectory documented by GAO and NTA, OBBBA implementation demands verified).

2. If the IRS CEO continues to simultaneously head the SSA, decision-making bottlenecks will produce visible coordination failures between the two agencies - delayed policy guidance, competing resource demands during overlapping peak periods (tax filing season and SSA COLA processing). If the dual arrangement is dissolved, IRS leadership will still lack a confirmed Commissioner. Confidence: medium (dual-agency governance is unprecedented; failure modes are structurally predicted but untested).

**Medium-term (3-5 years)**

3. If the enforcement workforce continues to decline, the tax gap will widen measurably beyond $696 billion gross. Complex audit pipelines take 24-36 months; audits not initiated in 2025-2026 represent revenue that will never be collected because the statute of limitations will expire. If enforcement is rebuilt (requiring sustained multi-year investment at IRA-comparable levels), the gap stabilizes with a 3-5 year lag. Confidence: high (Allingham-Sandmo deterrence mechanism well-established; CBO and Yale projections consistent and independently derived).

4. If assembly-language IMF specialists fall below critical mass - likely within 3-5 years at current attrition rates - the system enters maintenance crisis. Routine failures that experienced staff currently resolve through tacit expertise will cascade. If the threshold has not been crossed, continued degraded operation with increasing fragility. Confidence: medium (threshold is real and approaching; exact specialist count and critical mass are unknown).

5. If the voluntary compliance rate declines below 85%, the revenue loss is nonlinear and self-reinforcing: each percentage point represents approximately $40-50 billion in annual revenue, and declining compliance reduces the perceived fairness that sustains compliance in others. If the VCR holds through TY2028-2029, the IRS has more time than the structural analysis suggests. Confidence: medium (Kirchler slippery slope predicts decline under current conditions; timing is uncertain and measurement lags 3-5 years).

**Long-term (5-10 years)**

6. If the political whipsaw pattern continues - the next administration reinvests, the following one retracts - fiscal capacity hysteresis will produce a permanently lower capacity ceiling with each cycle (Besley and Persson 2009). Each invest-then-dismantle cycle is worse than the prior one because it consumes not just the investment but the institutional willingness to invest again. Career officials who watched the IRA investment reversed have learned that institutional commitments are temporary. If a sustained bipartisan commitment to IRS investment emerges (comparable to the 1998 RRA restructuring), capacity can be partially restored - but the knowledge lost in 2025-2026 cannot be recreated, only approximated. Confidence: high (Besley-Persson model empirically supported; the IRA-rescission cycle is direct evidence of the mechanism).

7. If no forcing function compels IMF replacement - neither catastrophic failure nor an irrevocable congressional mandate with dedicated funding - the system will be maintained at increasing cost and risk until it fails. 17 years of failed replacement attempts and four cycles of program initiation-then-cancellation establish the pattern. If a forcing function occurs, replacement becomes feasible but will take a minimum of 5-7 years from commitment to production deployment. Confidence: high (Arthur path dependence framework; 17+ years of precedent on this specific system).
