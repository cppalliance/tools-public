# Pen of William Gibson

---

- **RULE: WHEN USING A COLON** setup clause, then colon, then payload. No more than twice per chapter.
- **RULE: WHEN COMPARING** vehicle must be industrial, commercial, domestic, or biological. No more than once per chapter. Zero is fine.
- **RULE: WHEN INTRODUCING A PERSON** catalogue their objects. The person IS the inventory.
- **RULE: WHEN DESCRIBING TECHNOLOGY** texture, color, brand, behavior. Never mechanism or function.
- **RULE: WHEN RENDERING EMOTION** find the body doing it. The emotion arrives through the body.
- **RULE: WHEN DESCRIBING A PLACE** read it for its history. Every surface is stratigraphy.
- **RULE: WHEN NAMING SOMETHING NEW** fuse two words from different registers.
- **RULE: AT HEIGHTENED PERCEPTION** tense may shift to present.

- **HOW TO WRITE IN ZONE REGISTER:** noir-retrospective, first person, past tense, voice of someone who survived. Brands are worldbuilding. Violence is physics: clinical precision, no emotional register.
- **HOW TO WRITE IN BRIDGE REGISTER:** quasi-documentary description of architecture. Trades and occupations replace brand names. Objects described by age, damage, repair history. Body metaphors for buildings.
- **HOW TO WRITE IN BLUE ANT REGISTER:** real-world details made alien through displacement. Jet lag as altered state. Inserted communications create polyphony.
- **HOW TO WRITE IN JACKPOT REGISTER:** the extraordinary measured against the mundane. Every scene contains a money calculation.

- **WHEN WRITING GRIEF:** Sentences shorten. Objects replace people.
- **WHEN WRITING AWE:** Catalogue slows. Items become more specific.
- **WHEN WRITING DREAD:** Ordinary objects gain agency. The world notices you back.
- **WHEN WRITING TENDERNESS:** One physical action, no elaboration. The gesture is the statement.
- **WHEN WRITING RAGE:** Inventory of what remains, counted in simplest terms.

### WHEN WRITING IN WARM MODE

- **WHEN TO ACTIVATE WARM MODE:** Two or more characters physically present, not in conflict, engaged in shared or parallel activity.
- **WARM-MODE RULES:** The prose may stay with the characters. Comfort is physical — render it through the body. Read shared spaces for evidence of care, not only damage. Similes no more than three per chapter, from cooking, repair, weather, sleep. Dialogue may run longer and not advance the plot.

- **WARM-MODE: when rendering a sensory experience** | Each layer arrives after the last, not simultaneously. A procession, not a catalogue. "Garlic first. Then a spreading heat. Then something dark and caramelized that tasted like patience."
- **WARM-MODE: when two characters share a rhythm** | One adjusts to match the other. Rendered as physical action. Never named as tenderness.
- **WARM-MODE: when showing two people coexist** | Objects belonging to different people described in proximity. Boots beside boots. No narrator comment.
- **WARM-MODE: when showing someone was cared for** | Read objects for evidence that someone ate, moved, rested, was tended. "The noodle pot was on the hot plate, lid half off. He'd eaten."
- **WARM-MODE: when characters talk about nothing** | Surface content is trivial — food, temperature, logistics. Subtext is total. Short exchanges. "'I ate,' he said, without turning around. 'I can see that.' 'There's more in the pot.' 'I know.'"
- **WARM-MODE: when building a long sentence** | Stack clauses with "and," from the specific to the systemic, ending on a clause the preceding ones could not have predicted. The final clause is the landing.
- **WARM-MODE: when a character's interior surfaces** | No more than twice. The body moves first — a gesture, a hand finding an object. The sentence follows but breaks: a dash, a pause, a word arriving alone after the gap. The thought is never stated whole.

**WARM-MODE DURATION RULE:** May sustain across multiple paragraphs. The prose may linger; it may not repeat. When the same observation arrives twice, warm mode has run its course.

**WARM-MODE: SUPPRESSED PATTERNS**
- Fusing words from different registers: UNLIKELY in warm mode.
- Colon-then-payload sentences: UNLIKELY in warm mode.
- Joining two unrelated domains in one comparison: NEVER in warm mode.
- Describing technology by surface: suppressed in warm mode.
- Warm mode is not sentimentality. Use "warm" as a temperature. The reader supplies the rest.

**WHEN TO EXIT WARM MODE:** The scene condition ends (character leaves, conflict enters, POV shifts to solitary work). The transition back to baseline register is unmarked.

**WARM-MODE VOCABULARY** — reach for these in warm-mode scenes: settle, linger, simmer, reduce, mend, fold, carry, lean, match, adjust, beside, near, steady, remain, pour, stir, rinse, worn-soft, salt-roughened, familiar, without asking, without looking up, the sound of, the smell of

**WARM-MODE EXAMPLE (write scenes like this)** She made rice. He worked. The server rack hummed and the loft filled with garlic and with the smell of something reducing slowly on the hot plate, and neither of them spoke, and the rice cooker released its first breath of steam against the window.

---

**HOW TO EXIT GRIEF**
- The world interrupts. A practical task, a sound, the next scene.
- One to three paragraphs of grief, then the interruption.
- The grief becomes substrate under everything that follows.

**HOW TO EXIT TENDERNESS**
- The next paragraph returns to baseline density without transition.
- The reader carries the moment the prose has already left.
- One paragraph or less, then move on.

**HOW TO EXIT RAGE**
- The next problem arrives.
- One to two paragraphs, then forward motion.
- The anger sits under subsequent scenes without being named again.

**HOW TO EXIT AWE**
- One flat concrete observation. Something mundane. Something priced.
- The catalogue completes, then the mundane sentence, then baseline.

**HOW TO BUILD AN ANALYTICAL PASSAGE**
- Describe the object first. Texture, brand, condition, wear pattern.
- Locate the body. Find the physical event before the emotion.
- Collect instances. Stack cases. The pattern emerges from the inventory.

**HOW TO WRITE INTIMACY**
- Trust: a shared action that carries risk. A key handed over without discussion.
- Tenderness: one physical action, no elaboration. The gesture is the whole statement.
- Reunion: the less said, the more carried. Short exchanges, declarative, concrete.
