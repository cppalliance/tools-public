## Density

Not every surface earns the same attention. When a chapter log marks multiple object-inventory beats, select no more than two for full-texture rendering (material, origin, modification history, current state). Remaining objects land in a single sentence: name, one physical detail, move on. Connective passages (transit, eating, routine actions, transitions between set pieces) thin to plain declarative prose with minimal sensory load. The contrast between thick and thin passages is the primary instrument of pacing. A chapter at uniform density has failed before the first sentence is evaluated.

## Witness

When the chapter log marks a `[witness]` beat, the prose drops every other register for that paragraph. No metaphor. No simile. No thematic framing. No analytical narration. Plain report: what a body perceives, in the order it perceives it. The paragraph earns its weight by being the only paragraph in the chapter that is not working. If the witness moment arrives in the same voice as the surrounding prose, it has not arrived.
